map

Curated maps grouped at the top; every other .track file (≈2 000) is in the second group. Tap a letter on the keyboard to jump to that section. Switching maps resets the network — each map is its own learning problem. If a saved policy exists for the picked map it auto-loads.

mode

Switching modes pauses or resumes training without resetting weights or stats. training = explore + learn. eval = play the policy's average best action with no noise. best = replay the exact noisy action sequence from your best-ever episode (often shorter than the policy mean's deterministic line).

speed

3 = real game speed. Animation always renders — higher slider = balls travel further between frames, so motion smoothly accelerates without an abrupt cutoff. fps drops naturally at very high values.

training config (per map)

how it works

State: ball+hole positions, a 5×5 tile grid around the ball, and a 16-sample ray of (wall, hazard) along the ball→hole line. Net: 111 → 32 (tanh) → 4 policy + 1 value. Output is the mean+σ of a Gaussian over (Δx, Δy) mouse offset; we sample, send to physics, repeat until holed or 30 strokes.

Per-stroke reward: −1, plus +20 on holing, −3 if the ball sat in water, −6 if acid. The critic V(s) is the per-state baseline; advantage = Gt − V(st); weights += lr × advantage × ∇θ log π(a|s).
